Why Full Screen . Js?

When putting this project together, I wanted something that would let the photos speak for themselves, while still feeling smooth and interactive. I came across a tutorial and plugin that made it super easy to build a clean, responsive image gallery. What I liked most was how flexible it was: I could organize the photos in a way that felt natural, and the transitions added just the right touch of movement without being too flashy. It gave me the tools to highlight the different vibes of each place, like the warm coastlines of Puerto Rico, the colorful streets of Mexico, the city energy in Toronto, and the quiet beauty of Newfoundland.

Future Opportunities

This is just the beginning! Right now, it's a simple way to share my photography, but there's so much room to grow. I'd love to eventually add personal stories or sounds from each place, maybe even create an interactive map so people can explore by location. It could also become a shared space where others contribute their own photos and experiences tied to these places. With a few tweaks, it could even turn into a themed exhibition or a creative storytelling project. The framework is super adaptable, which makes it exciting to think about what else it could become.
